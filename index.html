How to Play — Round Sequence
1. Reveal Track Conditions — flip Weather + Hazard cards
2. Income Phase — collect Credits based on last race result
3. Market Phase — 5 new cards revealed from your deck. Buy, hire staff, claim Sponsor, bid in SHM
4. Workshop Phase — spend Workshop Capacity (WC) to install cards from your Workshop
5. Race (3 Segments) — Overheat Checks → Reckless declarations → Score each Segment
6. End Phase — award CP, fatigue recovery, refresh market
Segment Score Formula
Score = (Your Stat A − Demand A)
+ (Your Stat B − Demand B)
+ Reckless Bonus (+2 if declared)
+ Ability / Fuel bonuses
− Fatigue Tokens (−1 per token)
− Weather / Hazard penalties
Negative scores are normal early-season. Only the difference between players matters.
Workshop Capacity (WC) — How Installation Works
Step 1 — Buy: Pay Credits to take a card from your Market or SHM. It goes to your Workshop (garage area). It does nothing yet.
Step 2 — Install: During Workshop Phase, spend WC to install it. Minor cards = 1 WC. Major = 2 WC. S-Tier = 3 WC.
Step 3 — Active: Once fully installed, the card's stats immediately apply to your board.
Multi-round: If a card needs more WC than you have this round, place it in your Installation Area (WIP). Continue next round. Card is NOT active until complete.

Mechanic = 2 WC per round. You start with 1 Mechanic (2 WC). Hiring a 2nd Mechanic (5 Cr) gives you 4 WC/round — enough for 2 Major upgrades per round, or 1 S-Tier over 2 rounds.
Market System
Personal Market: At the start of each Race, your previous face-up cards are shuffled back into your deck, and 5 fresh cards are drawn. You see different cards each race.
Second-Hand Market (SHM): A shared 20-card deck of generic upgrades. 5 are auctioned face-up each Race. Any player can bid on any card. If you list a Workshop card for sale, the buyer pays you directly.
Engineer Deck Search: With an Engineer (7 Cr), you can pull any specific card from your full face-down deck once per race. This bypasses the 5-card random reveal entirely.

Hazards & Weather — What They Actually Do
Each Race reveals one Weather card and one Hazard card before racing begins. Both apply to every player equally — they are not chosen by anyone. Some upgrades can cancel specific Hazard effects (look for an "Ignore X" pill on a card).

Brake Fade (Hazard): all players take -1 to their Brakes margin during Segment 3 only. A card with the "Ignore Brake Fade" pill cancels this penalty for that player.
Safety Car (Hazard): Segment 1 awards no Championship Points or Credits to anyone, even the winner.
Yellow Flag (Hazard): Segment 2 still pays Credits for winning, but no Championship Point.
Tire Wear (Hazard): -1 Handling margin in Segment 3 for all players.
Track Evolution (Hazard): both Demand stats +1 in Segment 3 — the track gets harder as the race goes on.

Wet Track / Light Rain (Weather): +1 Handling Demand in every Segment. Quattro Grip ability and certain "Ignore Wet" cards cancel this.
Crosswind (Weather): your Aero stat is added as a 3rd scoring stat in every Segment this race — good for high-Aero cars, irrelevant otherwise.
Heatwave / Scorching (Weather): all cars gain extra Heat at race start, before any Overheat Check — raises everyone's risk of a forced check.

There are 15 Weather cards and 15 Hazard cards total — one of each is drawn randomly per Race. Most upgrade descriptions that mention "Ignore X penalty" are referring to one of these.
