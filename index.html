Rules Reference
Step 1 — Players & AI Opponents
Set each player as Human (you click their decisions) or AI (automatic). Mix and match — e.g. 1 Human vs 3 AI for solo play.
Step 2 — Race Calendar (always revealed BEFORE the car auction)
Study all 8 circuits before bidding on cars. A Technical-heavy calendar (Handling/Brakes segments) favours the MX-5 and Audi TT. High-Speed (Power/Aero) favours the Mustang and 370Z. Your car bid should respond to what's coming.
Step 3 — Car Auction
Cars are auctioned one at a time in random order. All players bid from their starting 12 Credits. Opening bid: 1 Credit. Highest bidder wins the car and pays that amount from their 12 Cr. Remainder is your Race 1 budget. AI players bid automatically.
Step 4 — Start Championship
Complete the auction to start.

How the Game Works
Each Round — 6 Phases in Order
1. Income — Collect Credits based on last race result
2. Fuel Station — Optionally swap fuel compound for this Race
3. Market Phase — Buy from your personal 8-card market, hire staff, claim Sponsor
4. Workshop Phase — Install upgrades, repair, use Engineer to search deck
5. Race (3 Segments) — Overheat Checks → Declare Reckless → Score each Segment
6. End Phase — Award position CP, Fatigue decay, reveal new Sponsor
Segment Scoring Formula
Score = (Your Stat A − Circuit Demand A)
+ (Your Stat B − Circuit Demand B)
+ Reckless Bonus (+2 if declared)
+ Ability / Sponsor / Fuel bonuses
− Fatigue Tokens (−1 per token held)
− Weather / Hazard penalties
Negative scores are completely normal early-season. Only the DIFFERENCE between players matters — the highest score wins the Segment.
Scout Deck (1 Credit) vs Engineer Deck Search — What's the Difference?
Scout Deck — 1 Credit, available to everyone
Your personal upgrade deck has 40+ cards shuffled face-down. Each round only 8 are revealed face-up for you to buy from.

Scouting lets you peek at the next 3 cards still face-down in your deck and reorder them in any sequence. Those cards then appear first in future market reveals — guaranteeing you see the upgrade you want rather than waiting for it to randomly surface.

Example: You Scout and see Brembo GT Kit, ECUTek Tune, Rear Wing. You move the Brembos to the top — they'll appear in next round's 8-card reveal.
Engineer Deck Search — requires Engineer hired
The Engineer browses your entire face-down deck and pulls out any specific card you want — bypassing the 8-card market reveal entirely. Pay the card's Buy cost. Take nothing and the action is free.

This is the key value of the Engineer (7 Credits to hire). Without one, you wait for cards to randomly appear in your 8-card market. With one, you target exactly what your build needs right now.

Example: You know you need the HKS GTIII Turbo Kit but it hasn't appeared yet. Deck Search finds it immediately.

Reckless Driving — Risk vs Reward
Declare Reckless before any Segment (simultaneous, face-down tokens).
Benefit: +2 to your Segment Score
Win bonus: +1 Credit extra if you win that Segment
Risk: Roll the red D6. Roll 1 or 2 = lose 1 Reliability
Counter: +1 to your Reckless Counter — never resets all season
Counter hits 5: gain 1 Driver Fatigue Token
Counter hits 8, 11, 14…: gain 1 more Token each
Each Fatigue Token: −1 to your score in every Segment this Race
Recovery:
End of any Race: −1 Fatigue Token
End of Race, finished 1st or 2nd AND zero Reckless: −2 Tokens
DNF: no recovery

Strategy note:
Reckless is efficient in Races 3–6 when you have Reliability buffer. Entering Race 7 or 8 with 2+ Fatigue Tokens costs 6–9 CP across three Segments — often more than the wins gained by going Reckless.
Cars at a Glance — Base Stats, Slots, and Achievements
Ability Cards — Choose 1 After Winning Your Car at Auction
After winning a car at auction, you immediately choose 1 of 5 Ability Cards specific to that car. Your ability is active for the entire Championship and cannot be changed. Effects triggered "after winning a Segment" are suppressed while you hold any Driver Fatigue Tokens.
